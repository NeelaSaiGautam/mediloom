Process for patient treatment

When a patient visits the hospital for treatment, the doctor first asks for their Patient ID and name to retrieve their medical history. To ensure security and privacy, the system generates a One-Time Password (OTP) and sends it to the patient's registered contact. The patient must provide this OTP to the doctor, who then enters it into the system to verify access. Once authenticated, the doctor can securely review the patient's previous health records, including past diagnoses, prescriptions, and test results, enabling informed and efficient medical decisions.

To ensure the security and integrity of patient data, cutting-edge technologies like blockchain are implemented. Blockchain provides a decentralized and tamper-proof ledger, ensuring that medical records remain secure and unaltered. Encryption techniques safeguard sensitive information, while multi-factor authentication (MFA) restricts unauthorized access. Smart contracts enable seamless and transparent data sharing between healthcare providers while maintaining strict access controls. These technologies collectively enhance data privacy, prevent breaches, and ensure trust in the healthcare system.

In addition to accessing the patient's previous medical history, the doctor can also update the system with the patient’s current medical condition. After diagnosis, the doctor can upload new prescriptions, test results, and treatment details to ensure that the patient’s records remain up to date. This allows for seamless continuity of care, enabling future consultations to be more efficient and well-informed.
